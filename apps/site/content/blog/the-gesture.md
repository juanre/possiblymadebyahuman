---
title: "The gesture"
date: 2026-05-28
summary: "What 'we cannot prove it, but here is us caring enough to show the work' actually means."
---

The product line we kept coming back to in design was:

> We cannot prove a human wrote it. This is us caring enough to show you anyway.

It is on the landing page, on every record page, and in the standing disclaimer rendered above the replay scrubber. It is the framing the rest of the system reports to.

"Caring enough to show the work" is doing real work in that sentence. It signals that the signer:

- chose to install a producer;
- chose to sign a session;
- chose to upload a record;
- chose not to delete it;
- chose to attach a URL to a piece of writing they cared about.

None of that is proof of authorship. All of it is a *gesture* — small, specific, voluntary, and verifiable.

What we wanted to avoid was the opposite gesture: stamping a single number on a piece of writing and letting a reader treat that number as the answer.

If the system works well, a reader looking at a record will come away with one of three reactions:

1. "This looks like ordinary editing — interesting, but unremarkable."
2. "This is mostly one big paste followed by a tiny edit — that is worth thinking about."
3. "I cannot tell from this record. The standing disclaimer is doing its job."

Any of those is the right answer. The wrong answer is "score: 0.83 human."
